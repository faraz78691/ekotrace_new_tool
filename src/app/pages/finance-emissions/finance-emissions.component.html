Investment

Financed Emissions

Emissions from investments made by the company, including equity investments, loans, and other financial assets. This category covers emissions from the activities of entities in which the company has invested.

This category divides financial investments into three types:
  1. Equity investments – Investment made the company using its own capital. It can be further subcategorised into: a) subsidiaries - where the company has financial control b) associate companies - where company has significant influence but no financial control c) joint ventures - where partners have joined financial control b) other investments - company has neither financial control nor significant influence eg. insurance contracts, credit guarantees.
  2. Debt investments – Corporate debt holdings held in the company’s portfolio, such as bonds, or commercial loans.
  3. Project finance - Long-term financing of projects (e.g., infrastructure and industrial projects) by the reporting company as either an equity investor (sponsor) or debt investor (financier)
To collect emissions data for investments made by the company, follow these steps:
  1. Decide which financial activity to include in the data collection (e.g., corporate lending, project finance, equity investments, bond underwriting)
  2. Identify company’s proportional share of investment in the investee or total project cost. As the investment portfolios are dynamic, companies should identify investments by choosing a fix point in time such as year-end.
  3. Determine the scope of emissions data needed e.g., Scope 1, Scope 2, Scope 3 (if significant)
  4. Relevant department for collecting the data – Corporate finance / Treasury team, Accounts department
How to use the tool for entering emissions data
  1. Choose the type of investment from the drop-down and then select the industry sector that most closely matches the sector in which investment has been done
  2. Select the calculation method
    • For Investment specific method - collect the scope1 and scope2 emissions data of the investee company for the reporting period and specify the proportional share of equity or project cost to calculate the emissions contribution of the reporting company.
    • For ‘Average data method’ – Input the investee company total revenue (for equity investment or debt investment when the project is in operational phase) or project construction cost if the project is still in construction phase in the reporting period. Specify the proportional share of equity or project cost to calculate the emissions contribution of the reporting company.
